The Preserver#

  • The Divine Name of Preserver and Human Accountability

Reading Objectives

By the end of this text, students will be able to:

  • Explain how the Name of Preserver (Al-Hafiz) is manifested in nature.

  • Describe the analogy of seeds and their growth.

  • Identify the connection between preservation in creation and the resurrection.

  • Explain why human actions will be accounted for in the Hereafter.

  • Reflect on personal responsibility and accountability.

Background#

In this passage from the 17th Flash, 15th Note, Bediüzzaman Said Nursi explains the manifestation of Allah’s Name Al-Hafiz (The Preserver) through examples from nature.

He reflects on the Qur’anic verses: “And whoever has done an atom’s weight of good shall see it, and whoever has done an atom’s weight of evil shall see it.” (99:7–8)

He argues that just as Allah perfectly preserves the identities and characteristics of countless seeds, He also preserves every human action. This preservation is evidence that people will be resurrected and held accountable on the Day of Judgment.

The passage connects observations from the natural world to the larger truths of faith, resurrection, and human responsibility.

Main Passage#

Download Main Passage

This is the verse And whoever has done an atom’s weight of good shall see it, And whoever has done an atom’s weight of evil, shall see it. (99:7-8) which indicates the fullest manifestation of the name of Preserver. If you want proof of this truth of the All-Wise Qur’an, look at the pages of the book of the universe, which is written on the pattern of the Clear Book; you will see the maximum manifestation of the divine name of Preserver and many things similar in many ways to the supreme truth of this verse.

For instance, take a handful of seeds of various trees, flowers, and plants, which are like the small coffers and are themselves all different and various, then bury them in the darkness of simple and lifeless earth. Then water them with simple water, which lacks balance, cannot distinguish things, and runs wherever you pour it.

Now come back in the spring, the arena of the annual resurrection, and look! Note carefully the time in the spring when the Israfil-like angel of thunder calls out to the rain as though sounding his trumpet, giving the good news of the breath of life being breathed into the seeds buried beneath the ground; you will see that under the manifestation of the divine name of Preserver, those seeds that resemble each other and are all mixed up and confused, conform perfectly and without error to the creative commands proceeding from the All-Wise Creator. They conform so exactly that in their growth a brilliant consciousness, insight, purpose, will, knowledge, perfection, and wisdom are apparent. For you see that those seeds which all resemble each other separate out and are distinguished from one another.

For example, this tiny seed has become a fig-tree, it has started to spread the All-Wise Creator’s bounties over our heads. It distributes them, stretching them out to us with its hands. And these two seeds which are superficially the same have produced the flowers called sunflowers and pansies. They have adorned themselves for us. They smile in our faces, making us love them. And this sort of seed has produced fine fruits; they became shoots, then trees. Whetting our appetites with their delectable tastes, scents, and forms, they invite us to themselves. They sacrifice themselves for their customers so that they may rise from the level of vegetable life to that of animal life. And so on.

You can make further examples in the same way. The seeds developed in such a way that the single handful became a garden filled with multifarious trees and flowers.

There was no fault, no error among them. They demonstrated the meaning of the verse, So turn your vision again; do you see any flaw? (67:3) Through the manifestation and bestowal of the name of Preserver, each of the seeds preserves and shows without confusion or defect the legacy inherited from its parent and origins.

This is a certain indication that the Preserver who carries out this wondrous work will demonstrate the supreme manifestation of His preservation at the resurrection of the dead and Last Judgement.

Yes, the manifestation of preservation that is thus faultless and without defect in insignificant, fleeting, transient states is a decisive proof that the actions, works, words, and good deeds and bad deeds of man, the holder of the Supreme Trust and vicegerent of the earth – deeds which have an eternal effect and supreme importance – are precisely preserved and will be subject to account.

Does man suppose he will be left to his own devices? God forbid! He is destined for eternity, and for everlasting happiness and perpetual misery. He will be called to account for all his actions, small and great, many and few. He will receive either reward or punishment. Witnesses to the maximum manifestation of preservation and to the truth of the first-mentioned verse are beyond count or calculation. Those we have shown in this Matter are a mere drop from the ocean, an atom from a mountain.

Glory be unto You! We have no knowledge save that which You have taught us; indeed, You are All-Knowing, All-Wise. (2:32)

Summary#

Bediüzzaman uses the example of a handful of different seeds buried in the soil. Although they appear mixed together and are watered by the same water, each seed grows into its own unique plant or tree without error.

This perfect preservation of identity demonstrates the manifestation of Allah’s Name Al-Hafiz (The Preserver).

He argues that if Allah preserves the identities of tiny seeds so precisely, He will certainly preserve every human action, word, and intention.

This serves as evidence that all people will be resurrected and called to account for their deeds. Human beings are not left without purpose; every action, whether good or bad, will eventually be rewarded or punished.

Glossary#

Word

Definition

Accountability

Being responsible for one’s actions and answerable for them.

Arena

A place where important events occur; here, the setting of spring and renewal.

Atom’s Weight

The smallest possible amount; even the tiniest action.

Bestowal

The act of giving or granting something.

Bounties

Blessings or gifts provided by God.

Coffer

A small chest or box used for storing valuable items; here, a metaphor for seeds containing future life.

Conform

To follow or obey a rule or pattern.

Consciousness

Awareness, understanding, or the ability to perceive and respond thoughtfully.

Delectable

Very pleasant or delicious.

Divine

Related to God.

Flaw

An imperfection or defect.

Hereafter

Life after death.

Israfil

The angel who will blow the trumpet announcing resurrection.

Legacy

Something passed down from a previous source.

Manifestation

A visible display or expression of something.

Mere

Small or insignificant in comparison; only or simply.

Multifarious

Having many different forms or varieties.

Pansy

A small flowering plant known for its colorful petals.

Perpetual

Continuing forever or for a very long time without stopping.

Preservation

The act of keeping something intact and protected.

Resurrection

Being brought back to life after death.

Supreme Trust

The special responsibility given to human beings by God.

Transient

Temporary; lasting only for a short time.

Vicegerent

A representative or steward; a person entrusted to care for the earth.

Whetting

Stimulating or increasing a desire or appetite.

Wisdom

The ability to act with deep understanding and good judgment.
